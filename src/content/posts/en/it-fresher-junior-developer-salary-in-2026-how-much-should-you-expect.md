### 1\. Why Salary Ranges Vary So Much in IT

If you've looked at job postings lately, you've probably noticed that two "junior developer" roles can list salaries that differ by 10–15 million VND per month. This isn't random. Several factors drive the variance, and understanding them helps you target the right opportunities and make a stronger case in negotiations.

**Tech stack matters more than title.** A junior developer working in Go, Kotlin, or cloud-native infrastructure commands a higher salary than one working in plain PHP or jQuery — not because one is smarter, but because demand outpaces supply in specialized stacks. Companies pay a premium for skills that are harder to find.

**Company type sets the ceiling.** A product company (one that builds and sells its own software) typically pays 20–40% more than a traditional outsourcing company for the same role. This reflects the higher business value of the product and the fact that product companies tend to invest more in retaining technical talent.

**English proficiency is a real multiplier.** In the Vietnamese IT market, candidates who can communicate confidently in English — not just read documentation but actually speak and write — consistently earn 15–25% more. This gap widens significantly if you pursue remote work for foreign clients.

**Portfolio quality trumps GPA.** A fresher with two deployed side projects and a GitHub with consistent commits will receive higher offers than one with a 3.8 GPA and no code in the wild. Employers are buying future output, not past grades.

### 2\. Fresher IT Salary by Role (Vietnam Market, 2026)

The following ranges represent typical monthly gross salaries (before tax) for freshers with 0–12 months of experience in the Vietnam market. These are broad market ranges — where you land within the range depends on the factors in Section 1.

#### By specialization:

**Frontend Developer (React, Vue):** 7,000,000 - 12,000,000 VND/month  
Demand remains strong, especially for React developers. Knowledge of TypeScript gives you an edge at the upper end of this range.

**Backend Developer (Node.js, Laravel, Spring Boot):** 7,000,000 - 13,000,000 VND/month  
Backend roles often start slightly higher than frontend due to the complexity of data handling, security, and system design considerations that are expected even at junior level.

**Fullstack Developer:** 8,000,000 - 13,000,000 VND/month  
Fullstack freshers who can genuinely handle both sides — not just claim to — tend to enter at the mid-to-upper range, especially at startups that need to move fast with small teams.

**Mobile Developer (React Native, Flutter):** 8,000,000 - 14,000,000 VND/month  
Mobile developers are in shorter supply, which pushes starting salaries slightly higher. Flutter developers have seen particular demand growth heading into 2026.

**QA / Test Engineer:** 6,000,000 - 10,000,000 VND/month  
QA roles typically enter lower than developer roles. However, freshers who understand automation testing (Selenium, Cypress, Appium) from day one can push toward the upper range quickly.

**DevOps / Cloud Engineer (Junior):** 9,000,000 - 15,000,000 VND/month  
This is the highest-starting track for freshers who have cloud certifications (AWS, GCP, Azure) or practical experience with Docker and CI/CD pipelines. Supply is genuinely thin at entry level.

**Data Analyst / Junior Data Engineer:** 7,000,000 - 12,000,000 VND/month  
Python proficiency, SQL competence, and familiarity with tools like Power BI or Tableau are the key differentiators in this range.

### 3\. Junior IT Salary by Role (Vietnam Market, 2026)

Junior typically means 1–3 years of real work experience — not internships, but paid positions where you shipped production code. The jump from fresher to junior compensation is significant and reflects the drop in hand-holding required from the senior team.

**Frontend Developer (React, Vue):** 12,000,000 - 20,000,000 VND/month

**Backend Developer (Node.js, Laravel, Spring Boot):** 13,000,000 - 22,000,000 VND/month

**Fullstack Developer:** 13,000,000 - 22,000,000 VND/month

**Mobile Developer (React Native, Flutter):** 14,000,000 - 24,000,000 VND/month

**QA / Automation Engineer:** 10,000,000 - 18,000,000 VND/month

**DevOps / Cloud Engineer:** 16,000,000 - 28,000,000 VND/month

**Data Engineer / ML Engineer (Junior):** 14,000,000 - 24,000,000 VND/month

At the junior level, the spread within each range widens. A junior backend developer at a product company with strong English and a track record of ownership can earn 22–25M, while the same title at a small outsourcing company might cap at 15M. Title alone tells you very little — context is everything.

### 4\. Salary by Company Type: Startup vs. Outsource vs. Product

Where you work often matters more than what you know when it comes to compensation at the junior level. Here's how the three main company types compare.

**Outsourcing companies** are the most common entry point for freshers in Vietnam. They offer stable work, structured teams, and relatively predictable salary bands. The trade-off is a salary ceiling that's noticeably lower than product companies — typically 10–30% less for the same role and experience level. Freshers at outsourcing firms can expect 7–12M; juniors cap around 15–18M in most cases unless they move into a team lead track.

**Product companies** (both Vietnamese and foreign-owned) pay the highest base salaries in the market. They invest heavily in hiring because their engineers directly impact the product's commercial success. Freshers at funded startups or established product companies can start at 10–14M; junior engineers with 2+ years can reach 20–28M. The catch: hiring bars are higher, interview processes are more rigorous, and competition is stiffer.

**Startups at early stage** are the most variable. A well-funded Series A startup may pay above-market to attract talent. A bootstrapped startup may offer below-market salary but compensate with equity, faster career growth, and broader responsibility. If you join early-stage, always clarify what "equity" actually means in practical terms before accepting.

**Foreign companies and branches** (e.g., Japanese, Korean, European tech companies operating in Vietnam) typically pay 15–25% above local market rates, but often have stricter cultural expectations around hierarchy and overtime norms. Language ability (English or Japanese) is a hard requirement for most.

### 5\. Ho Chi Minh City vs. Hanoi vs. Other Cities

Vietnam's IT job market is concentrated in two cities, and geography still affects compensation even in 2026 — though the gap has narrowed with remote-friendly policies becoming standard at many companies.

**Ho Chi Minh City** remains the highest-paying market for IT roles, driven by the highest concentration of product companies, foreign-owned tech firms, and funded startups. Salaries here run approximately 10–20% higher than the national average across all IT roles.

**Hanoi** is the second-largest market and has grown significantly in recent years, particularly in the banking tech, government IT, and outsourcing sectors. Salaries in Hanoi are typically 5–15% below Ho Chi Minh City for comparable roles, though the gap is smaller at senior levels.

**Da Nang, Can Tho, and other cities** have growing IT communities, particularly Da Nang which has attracted several outsourcing companies. Salaries are generally 15–25% below Ho Chi Minh City levels, but cost of living is also significantly lower. For freshers, joining a local company and working remotely for a Ho Chi Minh City or Hanoi-based employer is increasingly common and practical.

### 6\. Remote and International Salary Benchmarks

For Vietnamese developers who break into remote work for foreign clients — whether through platforms like Upwork, Toptal, direct contracts, or foreign company employment — compensation enters a completely different bracket.

**Freelance remote rates for junior developers (USD/hour):**

*   **Frontend / Backend (general stack):** $15 - $30/hour
*   **React / Node.js / Laravel specialist:** $20 - $40/hour
*   **Mobile (React Native, Flutter):** $25 - $45/hour
*   **DevOps / Cloud:** $30 - $55/hour

At 20 billable hours per week — a sustainable rate for a junior freelancer managing project scope and client communication — even the lower end of these rates produces a monthly income of $1,200–$1,600 USD, or roughly 30–40 million VND. This is 2–3x what most Vietnamese companies pay at the same experience level.

The prerequisite is strong English communication, a demonstrable portfolio, and the discipline to manage client relationships independently. These skills don't come overnight, but building toward them from day one puts you in a significantly stronger position by year two or three of your career.

### 7\. What Actually Moves Your Salary Up Faster

Waiting for annual review cycles is the slowest path to a higher salary. Here's what genuinely accelerates compensation growth in IT careers.

**Switching companies strategically.** In the Vietnamese IT market, the fastest salary jumps typically come from changing jobs — not from internal raises. A developer who stays at the same company for 3 years may see 8–12% annual increments. The same developer who moves once after 18 months and again after another 18 months can realistically double their salary in the same period.

**Moving up the value chain of skills.** Salaries follow scarcity. Learning cloud infrastructure, system design, or specialized frameworks like Go or Kotlin pushes you into a thinner talent pool and justifies higher offers. Broadening skills is good; deepening into high-demand niches is faster.

**Building a visible track record.** Open-source contributions, technical blog posts, speaking at local meetups, or maintaining a strong GitHub profile all signal credibility that salary negotiations alone can't manufacture. Several developers have negotiated 20–30% higher offers simply because the hiring team had already encountered their work before the interview.

**English proficiency investment.** Spending 6 months seriously improving business English — not just grammar but actual spoken fluency and writing clarity — has one of the highest ROIs of any skill investment a Vietnamese junior developer can make. The salary premium is immediate and persistent.

### 8\. How to Negotiate Your First IT Salary

Most freshers accept the first number offered because they don't want to seem greedy or risk losing the offer. This is a costly mistake — companies almost always have a budget range, and the first offer is rarely the top of that range.

**Do your research first.** Use ITviec, TopDev, LinkedIn salary insights, and community forums to build a realistic benchmark before the interview. Walking in with data is far more convincing than walking in with a feeling.

**Let them make the first offer.** If asked "What salary are you expecting?", it's acceptable to say: "I'd like to hear your budget range for this role first, so I can see if we're aligned." This is standard practice and not rude. Once they name a number, you have an anchor to work from.

**Negotiate on total compensation, not just base.** Benefits, remote flexibility, training budget, and faster review cycles are all negotiable and add real financial value. A 13M salary with a training budget of 5M/year, full remote, and a 6-month review cycle is meaningfully better than 14M with none of those things.

**The counter-offer script:** "Thank you for the offer. Based on my research into the market rate for this role and my experience with \[specific skill\], I was hoping we could discuss something closer to \[your target number\]. Is there flexibility there?" — calm, specific, and professional. Most hiring managers respect this more than immediate acceptance.

**Know your walk-away number.** Before every negotiation, decide the minimum you'll accept. This gives you clarity and prevents you from accepting an offer you'll resent two months in.

### Final Thoughts

Salary in IT is not fixed — it's a function of your skills, your market knowledge, your communication ability, and your willingness to advocate for yourself. The developers who earn the most at junior level are not always the most technically gifted; they're the ones who understood their own value clearly and knew how to communicate it.

Use the benchmarks in this guide as a starting point, not a ceiling. The Vietnamese IT market is growing fast, remote opportunities are genuinely accessible, and the gap between average and top-of-market compensation at junior level is large enough to make preparation and negotiation well worth the effort.
